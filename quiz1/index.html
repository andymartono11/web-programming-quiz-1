My Hometown: Surabaya

Surabaya, the capital of East Java province, is the second-largest city in Indonesia and a major economic hub. Known as the "City of Heroes," Surabaya holds a significant place in Indonesia's history due to its role in the Battle of Surabaya during the Indonesian National Revolution. The city is a fascinating blend of historical architecture, modern skyscrapers, and vibrant culture. Must-Try Local Food

Sate Madura

Delicious grilled chicken or lamb skewers served with a savory peanut sauce. A true classic of Javanese cuisine.

Rawon

A rich and flavorful beef stew, distinguished by its dark, almost black color from the use of 'keluak' nuts. It's often served with rice and sprouts.

Tahu Campur

A hearty mixed dish featuring fried tofu, meat, noodles, and a savory broth. It's a perfect blend of textures and flavors.

Top Tourist Places

Monkasel (Submarine Monument)

An actual retired Russian submarine that has been converted into a museum. A great place to explore naval history.

Suramadu Bridge

Connecting Surabaya with the island of Madura, this stunning bridge is an engineering marvel and offers a spectacular view at night.

Tugu Pahlawan (Heroes Monument)

A monument commemorating the heroes of the Battle of Surabaya. It's a must-visit for its historical significance and beautiful park.
